Understanding Mental Health

Mental health is as important as physical health. It affects how we think, feel, and act and helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.  

Common Mental Health Conditions:

Depression

Feeling sad or hopeless for extended periods.

Depression is a common mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a loss of interest in activities. People with depression may experience changes in appetite, sleep patterns, and energy levels. They may also have difficulty concentrating, making decisions, or feeling motivated.  
Anxiety

Feeling sad or hopeless for extended periods.Excessive worry and fear, often leading to physical symptoms.

Anxiety is a common mental health condition characterized by excessive worry and fear, often leading to physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, sweating, and difficulty breathing. People with anxiety may experience feelings of restlessness, irritability, and difficulty concentrating.
Bipolar Disorder

Experiencing extreme mood swings, from high (mania) to low (depression).

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings, including periods of mania (highs) and depression (lows). During manic episodes, people may feel overly excited, energetic, and have racing thoughts. During depressive episodes, people may feel sad, hopeless, and have low energy.  
Schizophrenia

A serious mental disorder involving hallucinations, delusions, and disordered thinking.

Schizophrenia is a serious mental disorder involving hallucinations, delusions, and disordered thinking. People with schizophrenia may see or hear things that aren't real (hallucinations) and believe things that aren't true (delusions). They may also have difficulty thinking clearly and organizing their thoughts.
ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)

Difficulty paying attention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity.

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. People with ADHD may have difficulty paying attention, sitting still, and controlling their impulses.  
OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

Persistent, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

OCD is a mental health disorder characterized by intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions). People with OCD may have persistent, unwanted thoughts that cause anxiety and distress. They may also engage in repetitive behaviors, such as handwashing, checking, or counting, to try to relieve their anxiety.
